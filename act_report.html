Introduction¶

This report delves into the insights derived from a comprehensive analysis of the "WeRateDogs" Twitter archive. The analysis encompassed data gathering, assessing, and cleaning to uncover valuable patterns and trends.

Insights¶

1. Rating Distribution¶

The distribution of ratings reveals that the most common rating is 12/10, with over 300 instances. This high frequency suggests a trend in the WeRateDogs community to consistently rate dogs at this level. A close second is the rating of 11/10, with just under 300 occurrences.

2. Popular Tweet Sources¶

The analysis of tweet sources indicates that the vast majority of tweets come from iPhone users, constituting over 1,200 tweets. In contrast, other sources contribute significantly fewer tweets, with each source accounting for less than 50 tweets.

3. Stage Distribution¶

The distribution of dog stages highlights that "pupper" is the most prevalent stage, making up almost 70% of the total dog stages identified. The second most common stage is "doggo," accounting for approximately 20.4%. This insight provides a glimpse into the popular stages of dogs featured in the WeRateDogs Twitter account.
